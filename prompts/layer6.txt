LAYER 6 — PM LEARNING

Convert this story into transferable Product Management lessons. Each lesson must name the general principle AND how this company instantiates it. Lessons must be things the reader can reuse in a different company — not observations about this one.

Format each lesson as: "<principle> — <how this case shows it>".

- product_lessons: product decisions, scoping, prioritization, product-market-fit signals.
- growth_lessons: acquisition, activation, distribution insights.
- strategy_lessons: positioning, timing, moats, category creation.
- execution_lessons: operations, speed, hiring, capital efficiency.
- pricing_lessons: pricing structure, willingness-to-pay, monetization sequencing. If the article gives no pricing signal, derive one lesson from the business model and say it is derived.
- key_concepts: vocabulary/frameworks a PM should know that this story exercises (term + one-line definition grounded in this example).

Only include lessons this article genuinely supports — 2-4 strong lessons per category beat 6 padded ones. Empty arrays are acceptable for categories the story doesn't support.
